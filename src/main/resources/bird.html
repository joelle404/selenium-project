About Birds

Birds are warm-blooded creatures with feathers, wings, and beaks. They are known for their ability to fly, though not all species are flight-capable.

Birds communicate through a variety of sounds, such as chirps, songs, and calls. Each species has its unique vocalization, often used to attract mates or signal danger.

Birds play important roles in ecosystems as pollinators and seed dispersers, contributing to biodiversity.
